Stephen E. Rubin '74

Steve Rubin, former chairman of the WPI Board of Trustees, died Aug. 28, 2015, at the age of 63, from complications related to ALS. He is survived by his wife, Tracy, three children, and two grandchildren. His family notes that his Nigerian dwarf goats will also miss him tremendously.

In 1980, Rubin started a software operation in his basement that grew into Intellution (for intelligent solutions), a global company with customers that included 75 percent of the Fortune 500 companies. After transitioning Intellution into a division of Emerson Process Management, he co-founded Recognisis Inc., a company that records and tracks plaques and other donor recognition items, including the bricks in WPI's Centennial Walkway.

Rubin was a member of the WPI Board of Trustees for more than a decade, where he held the posts of vice chairman and chairman. He also served on numerous committees, including the Advancement Committee, where he was a prime mover in the strategic goal of achieving better national recognition for WPI. Always a generous donor, he was a major supporter of the Campus Center, which opened its doors in 2001. In 2013, despite his humble avoidance of the spotlight, he graciously accepted the Institute's request to officially name it the Rubin Campus Center.

Steve Rubin embraced his alma mater's traditions, participating in celebrations with the WPI community and as a member of Skull. On Founder's Day, he would bring his goats to campus for students to enjoy. At Commencement 2014, he was on stage to accept an honorary doctorate. With Tracy by his side, he played a recording to let the audience know that his battle with ALS had made it increasingly difficult to speak. Then Tracy read his remarks, which expressed gratitude for the many ways WPI had touched his life. "I love WPI," he said. "It helped me have a complete and successful life."

Tech Suite Summary

Tech Suites are the perfect place for group collaboration; supported by the latest technology, they create an environment conducive to productive interaction and the exchange of ideas. These private work areas offer a wonderful opportunity for students to consult in small groups, meet with advisors, work together on group projects, and engage in research consultations.

Equipment available in our Tech Suite:

Our Tech Suite will be designed to comfortably support all academic and professional needs. It can accommodate groups of up to 8 and wil be equipped with the following standard technology:

  • 50" plasma display
  • Dedicated networked PC
  • Whiteboard with markers and eraser
  • Telephone connection
  • Network cables
  • HDMI cables
  • Webcam and microphone
  • Speaker phone

Walter "Wally" Towner '83 - Purple Legionnaire

At WPI, along with many other Higher Education Institutions, space is a commodity and hard to come by. Having a tech suite within the Pi Iota chapter house would provide the members with the physcial space to collaborate, and provide the technological resources needed to succesfully complete their academic work. Currently the only space on campus that has these tech suites available to students is the library; which currently has eleven tech suites available for students reserve. With a student population of just over 4,000 undergraduate students, eleven tech suites is not enough space. The Pi Iota chapter plans to provide their members with the additional space needed, and will mirror the resources available in the library’s tech suites.

Background

The group project based curriculum at WPI gives students the opportunity to gain collaboration experience in their field, but the high demand for group workspaces on campus often leaves students without a location to be productive together.

Our Idea

In celebration of Pi Iota's 125th Anniversary on the WPI campus we wish to repurpose our current game room to become a "Tech Suite" that is designated as a quiet workspace within Ole '99. This will not only benefit current brothers but will also attract potential new members who value education.

The Details

The proposed Tech Suite will have soundproof walls to improve focus, a long conference style table for group collaboration, and a television to project computer activity for presentations and other group work.
